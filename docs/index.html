PROJECT
Medicus Albus

ROLE
Consultant, Overseer
CONTRIBUTION
Field experimentation, operationally-relevant expertise, baseline aetherological theory, subject selection

STATUS
Late-stage field testing

Developed alongside Project Resonant, Project Medicus Albus sought to create a Magitek catalyst which could artificially enhance the abilities of an experienced wielder to levels far beyond their individual capacity by drawing upon and assisting in converting surrounding ambient aether.

Named for the role it primarily wished to emulate, Project Medicus Albus sought to empower a select few conjurers within the Garlean ranks to practice their powers at a level rivaling that of the White Mages of eras past, the practice (as known) presently living on only through the Padjal.

A small handful of these prototype catalysts were produced and distributed, and through guided practice, its wielders began to produce the desired results. However, progress unofficially halted as many key founding personnel came to be lost and scattered throughout the XIIth Legion's defeats.

DETAILS
PROJECT
Resonant

ROLE
Subordinate Researcher
CONTRIBUTION
Aetherological theory, genome mapping

STATUS
Completed, reopened

The flagship project of Aulus mal Asina, for which he earned the patronage of the Crown Prince himself, sought to overcome the fundamental shortcoming of the Garlean race: their genetic inability to manipulate aether.

Seeking to identify and correct this inhibition, Project Resonant and its offshoot avenues of investigation led to many fantastic advances in the fields of genetics, synthetics, and aetherochemistry, and though tangible results could ultimately be obtained through genetic alteration, those who survived the modifications succumbed to psychosis. While this had its uses for military applications, in terms of the original aim of the project, it was deemed unacceptable.

Success did not come until mal Asina shifted his focus from body to soul, forsaking genetic modification to instead seek to artificially imprint a facsimile of the Echo upon his subjects. Requiring a host template and a considerable score of Hypertuned to act as aether conduits, the process proved unstable, even deadly, until a final breakthrough saw Fordola rem Lupis emerge as the first successfully half-infused agent, with Zenos yae Galvus becoming himself the second success and first full infusion.

While the fruits of the project are undeniably potent and have delivered an outcome surpassing the intent to merely "level the playing field," subordinates of the late mal Asina have reopened the project in the hopes of achieving its original goals: namely, to allow Garleans command of aether by way of genetic modification rather than bypassing the body by augmenting the soul; a process hoped to be far more commonly feasible than the last.

DETAILS
PROJECT
Armis Aucta Aether

ROLE
Director, Lead Researcher
CONTRIBUTION
Aetherological theory, developmental direction, basic Magitek engineering, subject selection, oversight of field implementation

STATUS
Early-stage field testing

Building upon the concepts explored in Project Medicus Albus, Project Armis Aucta Aether, as the name plainly states, was conceived with the intent to develop aether-augmented arms and armor. Again designed to amplify the natural abilities of the wearer, this project also incorporated devices capable of not only siphoning and transforming aether, but also condensing and storing it, allowing the wielder to unleash significant bursts of power during short- term engagements.

Additionally, subjects selected to participate in field tests of the Armis products are those displaying rare genetic combinations and abnormalities, as it is the ultimate intent that the knowledge gained through Projects Medicus Albus and Armis Aucta Aether might be used to supplement the renewed aims of Project Resonant, providing a Magitek-based advantage which would set aether- wielding Garleans clear above their Eorzean counterparts.

While the project is ongoing, difficulty acquiring funding and obtaining the relevant training for its field agents, as well as being overshadowed by more urgent tasks, has significantly slowed progress.

DETAILS
PROJECT
Scutum Animae

ROLE
Director, Lead Researcher, Lead Engineer
CONTRIBUTION
Aetherological theory, object analysis, Magitek engineering, field experimentation

STATUS
Completed

Following the decimation of the Garlean capital at the hands of an eikon, the continued threat of tempering proved a tremendous obstacle to those who sought both to investigate the incident and provide relief to the citizens and soldiers still active underground and within the outskirts.

The Ilsabard Contingent of the Eorzean Alliance had arrived equipped with devices, called Warding Scales, which were capable of shielding the bearer from eikonic influence. Created by Hannish alchemists, these scales were so named for having been crafted using the scales of a dragon. Aside from their inherent rarity, the complex alchemical techniques necessary for their creation would prove difficult to replicate outside of Thavnair.

Project Scutum Animae was undertaken when one of these scales was recovered in the wake of an ambush, and through careful examination, formulation, and fabrication, functionally identical Magitek recreations of the technology were produced. Making use of an intricate and optimized combination of an aetherospectrometer, aetherotransformer, and aetheroprojector, the device is able to detect dangerous concentrations of aether and neutralize them in real time by producing oppositely-formulated energies. As this serves only to de-aspect incoming aether, however, it serves as a poor defense against traditional magick assaults and cannot protect the user from anything but tempering influences. Fueled by ceruleum, with a single-tank operating window approaching three suns under mid-high load, they provide a viable alternative which Garlean industrial technology could see manufactured and distributed.

Though the prototype devices flourished during field testing, they proved irrelevant in the context of the Skyfall, and need for them diminished nigh entirely before mass-production could be considered. Nevertheless, the project is regarded as a total success and the technology remains available for ready use as needed. One lesser but still potent application noted is the potential to protect a user from ambient aetherpoisoning, as experienced at large battlefields and gravesites and within certain crystal fields.

DETAILS
PROJECT
Inoculationem

ROLE
Director, Lead Researcher
CONTRIBUTION
Aetherological theory, data collection, aetheric formulation

STATUS
First-stage completion

The most dangerous aspect of eikons beyond their raw destructive power has ever been tempering, the influence by which the very souls of its victims are twisted, imprinted, and molded to match the will of the performing eikon. It was an unfortunate reality that even the Allagan Empire, (as far as history knows,) could not overcome. Yet the emergence of the Eorzean Contingent's Warding Scales, and the success of Project Scutum Animae following, provided a new and heretofore unique opportunity to withstand the influence of an eikon without a need for the exceedingly rare gift of the Echo.

In being able to approach an eikon without worry of being tempered, it has become possible to collect and catalogue the magnitude and composition of the selfsame aetheric influences responsible for tempering. While the data gathered would only be relevant to a singular summoning of a specific eikon, it would theoretically allow for the formulation of an equal and opposite aetheric influence, which would thereby allow one to reverse tempering in a subject and restore their soul towards a recoverable equilibrium.

Project Inoculationem sought to do precisely this and found success in its flagship case, but before it could be applied widescale, the Eorzean Alliance unveiled and implemented a much more versatile tempering cure of its own. This method, however, utilizing a summoned demi-eikon, is believed to be inherently imprecise and therefore dangerous, as it is highly likely to merely be another manner of tempering which leaves the victim susceptible to the whims of the summoner responsible for their treatment. As the Inoculationem method is mathematically formulated from empirical data, it is in every way preferable. Most importantly, it may be enacted within any capable Garlean facility without the need to rely upon one who is both gifted in aether and familiar with the parent demi-eikon.

Given the aforementioned shortcomings of the Eorzean method, the scope of the project has expanded with the aim to measure the influence of the demi-eikon so that any victims of its imperfect procedure may be restored correctly, with the ultimate goal being to restore all citizens of Garlemald accordingly. In tandem with this and Project Scutum Animae, Garlemald will be innoculated against suffering a repeated fate of the horrible destruction which shattered the seat of the Empire and brought catastrophic ruin to the capital city.

DICASTERIUM DE PROGRESSIO
MAGITECI ET AETHEROLOGIA

The Department of Magitek Development and Aetherology (DPMA) is the research branch of the XIIth Legion, formed with the induction and appointment of its head, Aulus mal Asina, in service to the Legatus himself, Crown Prince Zenos yae Galvus, in pursuit of its flagship undertaking, Project Resonant.

Despite Project Resonant being the primary purpose of the DPMA, many secondary projects were contributed both by mal Asina and his subordinates. Aside from advancements in hypertuning technology, mal Asina and his subordinates may claim credit for novel breakthroughs in synthetic Magitek forms, as seen in the widespread Roaders, renowned for their uncanny resemblance to organic flesh.

The DPMA has operated in both the East and West, beneath Doma Castle and within Gyr Abania, where experimentation upon the local populations has caused a loss of life well into the thousands.

INTERIORI REVIDERE

The Department of Interior Review, most commonly referred to as "Interior Review" (IR) is the manner of self-governance within the Legion which investigates all incidents, allegations, and suspicions of criminal or professional misconduct. While IR may sometimes cooperate with the Milites Stationarii, their primary concerns encompass the clerical functions of the Legion and its bureaucratic framework to also include the DPMA.

LABORATORIUM
AETHEROLOGICUM

The Aetherology Laboratory, most often referred to simply as "The Laboratorium," is a clandestine facility rumored to exist within the sublevels of the Garlean Consulate in Kugane. While such a place may theoretically trouble Garlean relations with the Hingan bakufu, the diplomatic rights afforded deny any unwelcome search of the premises. As a result, the best official response to any allegations must ever be "out of sight, out of mind."

In full acknowledgement of this reality, Laboratorium personnel must exercise due caution to ensure that their activities remain plausibly benign, and the procurement of subjects for experimentation (if such ever occurs) must be carried out with incredible discretion.

Yet, the existence of the Laboratorium itself remains largely an unsubstantiated rumor, as the brave few who have managed to enter the Consulate in search of it, even while having managed to identify a number of personnel suspected to work there, have never succeeded in locating it.

MILITES STATIONARII

Stationed Soldiers refer to those assigned to the role of military police within the Legion. Though it is traditional for legionaries to be assigned as Stationarii only temporarily, often as a stepping stone early in their careers, they are assigned by specific station, and the stations themselves are fixtures as permanent as Garlean control over the associated territory.

SEGMENTUM ORIENTALE

The Eastern Segment refers to the portion of the XIIth Legion which remained behind to oversee Doma and Yanxia while much of its might traveled west to stabilize the former XIVth Legion territories of Ala Mhigo and Gyr Abania.

VIRTUTE IN GLORIA

A common phrase that has become the trademark of the XIIth Legion's Segmentum Orientale. Meaning "Virtue in Glory," it is an homage to the Legion's emphasis on martial excellence, the pursuit of power, and reverence for fierce-won victory.
